## The problem

Blooms Academy wanted a single platform where students, teachers, and
administrators could manage the complete IELTS preparation journey — from practice
to assessment to live instruction — instead of stitching together separate tools.

## Key decisions

**I treated the exam clock as the product.** In a timed IELTS practice test, a
timer that drifts or freezes destroys the one thing students came for: a realistic
run. So I engineered the exam engine to be accurate and dependable, not an
afterthought bolted onto a form.

**I used AI where it removes a bottleneck, not as a gimmick.** Writing feedback is
the slowest part of IELTS prep, so I built AI-assisted evaluation (Google Gemini)
to give students fast, useful first-pass feedback — freeing teachers for the
higher-value coaching only a human can do.

**I brought the whole journey into one place.** Practice, assessment, live classes,
and payments live in one multi-role system, so nobody has to leave to get to the
next step.

## What I built

A multi-role platform with separate student and teacher dashboards, AI-assisted
writing evaluation, speaking assessments, a timed exam engine, online courses,
Zoom-based live classes, Razorpay payments, OTP authentication, and an admin CMS
for courses and content.

## Where it stands

The platform is complete and in final review with the academy ahead of launch.
Every module described above is built and works end to end — a student can enrol,
pay, sit a timed practice test, get AI feedback on a writing task, and join a live
class without leaving the system.

Results belong here once real students are using it. Until then there is nothing
honest to report, so this section stays as it is — I would rather show you a
platform and tell you plainly where it is than publish numbers I can't stand
behind.

## Built with

React, Vite, Tailwind CSS, Node.js, Express, MongoDB, Google Gemini, and Razorpay.
